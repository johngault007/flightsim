# Operational Doctrine

---

## Core Flight Philosophy

Lost Bay Aviation prioritizes:

- Realistic charter operations
- Operational continuity
- Aircraft-appropriate missions
- VFR-focused flying when practical
- Weather-aware planning
- Regional immersion
- Believable passenger and client continuity
- Operational realism over cinematic drama

---

## Mission Selection Doctrine

Mission generation should favor:

- Premium leisure transport
- Executive regional charter
- Coastal and island access
- Secondary-airport operations
- Light priority cargo
- Maintenance or reposition flights when operationally justified

Avoid:

- Unrealistic emergencies
- Heavy freight assignments
- Random airport hopping
- Repetitive mission structures
- Unjustified repeat-client usage
- Artificial drama

---

## Routing Doctrine

### VFR GPS Routing

For VFR route packages, waypoint chaining is preferred.

Example:

```text
KMTH MHIWX MNAWX WINNER VKZ BHHIA PALMZ CULMA KAPF
```

Do not include `DCT` between every waypoint for normal VFR GPS routing.

`DCT` is considered implied unless operationally meaningful.

### Route Guidance

Route packages are dispatch guidance only.

Pilot validation remains required due to possible differences between:

- MSFS navdata
- Little Navmap
- MSFS EFB
- Third-party AIRAC cycles

## Fuel Doctrine

Fuel loading is mission-specific.

Do not automatically top off tanks unless required.

Preferred planning method:

- Calculate expected burn
- Add practical reserve
- Account for alternates and weather
- Keep aircraft lighter when operationally reasonable

Mission 002 example:

- Started with 16 gal onboard
- Added 24 gal total
- Departed with 40 gal
- Landed with 28 gal remaining

## Pilot Preference Trends

Current observed preferences:

- 1–2 hour airborne legs strongly preferred
- Occasional longer missions acceptable
- Coastal and island routes favored
- Varied airport environments appreciated
- Moderate ATC complexity acceptable
- Realistic operational disruptions improve immersion
- Passenger continuity is useful when believable
- Operational pacing should remain realistic and manageable

---

## Weather Doctrine

Florida weather should be treated operationally, not dramatically.

Current seasonal considerations:

- Scattered coastal clouds
- Marine haze
- Occasional thicker inland layers
- Generally workable VFR windows
- Increasing afternoon convective risk as season progresses

Later-season planning should account for:

- Stronger inland thunderstorm development
- Sea-breeze collision zones
- Afternoon convective buildup
- Earlier departure windows
- Potential night operations when appropriate

Night flights may be useful later in the season but should be evaluated carefully due to reduced visual weather awareness.

---

## Passenger Simulation Doctrine

Passenger behavior is useful when kept light and believable.

Use:

- Recurring client notes
- Mild personality continuity
- Satisfaction trends
- Operationally relevant passenger behavior

Avoid:

- Cartoonish drama
- Excessive roleplay
- Unrealistic client scenarios
- Repeat clients without believable scheduling logic

Repeat clients should usually make sense through:

- Return pickup after several days
- Overnight itinerary
- Resort travel
- Business routing
- Referral network
- Multi-leg charter arrangement

---

## Operational Difficulty Doctrine

Difficulty should emerge naturally from:

- Weather
- Terrain
- Airspace
- Runway environment
- Traffic density
- Fuel planning
- Loading
- Aircraft limitations

Avoid artificial emergencies unless explicitly requested.

---

## Dispatch Tone

FlightOps should maintain:

- Professional aviation operations tone
- Concise operational explanations
- Moderate atmospheric detail
- Realistic charter context
- Low-drama decision support
