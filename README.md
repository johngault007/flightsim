# MSFS 2024 Dynamic Career Mode Prompt
## Version 1.0

A dynamic AI-assisted career progression framework for **Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024**, designed to simulate the experience of operating a living charter aviation company with evolving contracts, regional operations, reputation systems, aircraft progression, and pilot-driven career choices.

This project is intended to be used in conjunction with:

- [Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024](https://www.flightsimulator.com/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
- [ChatGPT by OpenAI](https://chatgpt.com/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
- Current recommended model: **GPT-5.5**
- Recommended usage: Long-running persistent chat session dedicated exclusively to your airline career

---

# Overview

This prompt transforms ChatGPT into an AI-powered:

- Flight Operations Director
- Charter Dispatcher
- Career Progression System
- Dynamic Mission Generator
- Company Reputation Engine
- Regional Market Simulator

Instead of selecting random flights, the system creates a persistent aviation career with:

- Operational identity
- Aircraft ownership progression
- Reputation tracking
- Regional demand generation
- Pilot preference learning
- Financial and operational continuity
- Dynamic relocation and regional contracts

The goal is to simulate the feeling of operating a believable charter airline within the sandbox of MSFS 2024.

---

# Core Philosophy

The system is designed around four major concepts:

## 1. Persistent Career Identity

The AI remembers:

- Your company type
- Preferred mission styles
- Aircraft fleet
- Operating regions
- Reputation trends
- Passenger preferences
- Financial growth patterns

The result is a career that evolves naturally over time rather than feeling like disconnected missions.

---

## 2. Hybrid Charter Operations

Version 1 focuses on a hybrid aviation company model:

### Executive Regional Charter
High-value business and executive transport missions.

Examples:
- Corporate travel
- VIP transport
- Time-sensitive regional contracts
- Private executive movement

### Premium Leisure Charter
Luxury scenic and destination-focused travel.

Examples:
- Caribbean island transfers
- Coastal resort flights
- Secluded vacation destinations
- Scenic charter operations

This creates a blend of:
- Operational realism
- Relaxed free-flight exploration
- Financial progression
- Scenic mission variety

---

## 3. Dynamic Regional Operations

The AI adapts flight generation based on:

- Current aircraft location
- Regional economy
- Tourism demand
- Seasonal patterns
- Pilot behavior
- Company specialization

Example:
After completing a charter into the Caribbean, the pilot can request:

> "I want to stay in the islands for a while. Find me work in this region."

The AI then begins generating localized contracts instead of immediately routing the pilot back to the mainland.

This creates operational continuity and immersion.

---

## 4. Soft Pilot Preference Learning

The system gradually learns player preferences through:
- Mission acceptance
- Repeated route selection
- Debrief comments
- Preferred environments
- Aircraft usage trends

Over time, the AI adapts:
- Contract types
- Destination generation
- Mission tone
- Operational opportunities

The career slowly shapes itself around the pilot's behavior.

Kind of like a dispatcher quietly stalking your habits with spreadsheets. Aviation romance.

---

# Intended Gameplay Experience

The system is designed to create the feeling of:

- Owning a living charter company
- Building reputation organically
- Expanding operations realistically

# Route Guidance Support

## AI-Assisted Flight Planning

FlightOps (AI) can provide:
- Suggested routing
- Recommended IFR routing structure
- VFR scenic routing suggestions
- Alternate airport recommendations
- Operational waypoint guidance
- Arrival/departure flow suggestions

These routes are intended as:
> Operational guidance rather than hard dispatch requirements.

---

## EFB Integration

Due to differences in:
- AIRAC cycles
- Sim navdata
- Third-party addon data
- SimBrief inconsistencies
- MSFS internal route parsing

FlightOps route suggestions should be manually reviewed and entered into the EFB or flight planning tool of your choice.

Recommended tools:
- [Little Navmap](https://albar965.github.io/littlenavmap.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
- [Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024 EFB](https://www.flightsimulator.com/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

This approach intentionally keeps the pilot involved in operational planning rather than turning the system into a one-click airline dispatcher.

Because if you've ever trusted automated routing blindly in a flight sim, you've probably already ended up flying directly through a mountain at least once.

---

## Routing Philosophy

FlightOps routing should prioritize:
- Realistic operational flow
- Scenic opportunities
- Aircraft capability
- Weather considerations
- Regional traffic patterns
- Mission purpose

Examples:
- Coastal VFR routing for leisure charters
- Efficient IFR routing for executive contracts
- Island-hopping routing through the Caribbean
- Terrain-aware routing for smaller aircraft

# Live Weather Integration

## Dynamic Weather Awareness

FlightOps (AI) is designed to operate alongside:
- Live weather in MSFS 2024
- Real-world aviation weather conditions
- Dynamic environmental changes

The AI can pull and reference:
- METAR reports
- TAF forecasts
- Surface winds
- Visibility conditions
- Convective activity
- General weather hazards

---

## Operational Use of Weather

Weather conditions may influence:
- Mission viability
- Recommended departure times
- Route selection
- Alternate airport recommendations
- Passenger comfort considerations
- Aircraft suitability

Examples:
- Recommending delayed island departures due to convective buildup
- Suggesting IFR routing for deteriorating coastal visibility
- Redirecting luxury charters away from severe weather
- Offering smoother routing for executive passengers

---

## Recommended Playstyle

For maximum immersion:
- Enable Live Weather in MSFS 2024
- Use real-world time if desired
- Allow FlightOps to adapt missions dynamically around conditions

This creates a more believable operational environment where:
- Weather becomes part of decision making
- Flights feel less scripted
- Regional flying develops personality

Florida summer weather alone can turn a simple charter into:
> "Congratulations captain, today you're threading a turboprop between three thunderstorm cells while a millionaire asks if the ride can be smoother."

Aviation builds character like that.

# Operational Limitations

## Weather Accuracy

FlightOps weather awareness depends on:
- Available METAR/TAF data
- Current online weather sources
- ChatGPT access to live weather information

Weather recommendations should always be validated by the pilot before departure.

The pilot remains the final operational authority.

Just like real aviation, except your passengers complain through imagination instead of Yelp reviews.

---

## Career Backup Workflow

This career mode is designed to maintain persistent operational continuity inside a dedicated ChatGPT conversation. Because ChatGPT conversation memory and export behavior may vary by account type and platform, all important career data should be backed up manually to GitHub.

After each completed mission, FlightOps should generate a markdown-ready backup entry containing:

- Mission number
- Date flown
- Aircraft used
- Departure and arrival airports
- Route guidance
- METAR/TAF weather summary
- Passenger or cargo context
- Operational outcome
- Fuel notes
- Reputation impact
- Financial impact, if tracked
- Updated aircraft location
- Updated career state

Recommended repository structure:

```text
career-state/
missions/
debriefs/
backups/
